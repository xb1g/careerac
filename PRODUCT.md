# Product

## Register

product

## Users

California community college students planning their transfer to 4-year universities (primarily UCs and CSUs). Many are first-generation, working part-time, and navigating the transfer process with minimal guidance. They use CareerAC during high-stress moments — late at night, between classes, or when registration deadlines loom — to get clarity on which courses to take and when.

## Product Purpose

CareerAC is an AI-native transfer planning platform that eliminates the hours of manual research students currently endure. It generates personalized semester-by-semester transfer plans through natural conversation, helps students recover when courses fail or plans change, and surfaces verified transfer stories from peers who've succeeded. Success means a student goes from confusion to a actionable plan in under 5 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Confident, clean, approachable. We know the transfer system inside-out and communicate that expertise without arrogance. The interface feels like a capable friend who happens to be a transfer counselor — precise when it matters, warm when it counts. Never clinical, never cutesy.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered, overwhelming dashboards with dense data tables and excessive chrome
- Generic SaaS template aesthetics (identical card grids, gradient text, big-number hero metrics)
- Academic/university portal designs that feel bureaucratic and dated
- Overly playful or gamified interfaces that undermine the seriousness of academic planning

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity under pressure** — The interface must make sense at 11pm when a student is panicking about registration. Every screen has a clear primary action and no ambiguous states.

2. **Progress, not paperwork** — Transfer planning feels like bureaucratic homework everywhere else. CareerAC makes it feel like forward momentum. Show the path, hide the complexity.

3. **Expert confidence, human warmth** — AI-generated plans should feel authoritative and trustworthy, not robotic. Use precise language but conversational tone. Celebrate milestones.

4. **Recovery is normal** — Failed a class? Changed your major? The UI should make replanning feel like a natural continuation, not a catastrophic reset. No shame, just solutions.

5. **Density with breathing room** — Students need to see their full plan at a glance, but not be overwhelmed. Linear-inspired data density with intentional whitespace and clear hierarchy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Color-blind safe status indicators (not color-only)
- Readable at 200% zoom
- Keyboard-navigable plan interactions
